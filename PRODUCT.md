# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Tourists (travelers) planning and booking travel in East Africa (primarily Uganda). They browse on mobile and desktop, often with a specific destination or activity in mind. Vendors (local businesses: hotels, tour operators, restaurants, transport) manage listings. Admins oversee the platform.

## Product Purpose

DirtTrails connects travelers with local East African tourism businesses: hotels, tours, activities, restaurants, transport, flights, shops, and events. Success means a tourist can discover, compare, and book a service in under 3 minutes with confidence that the listing is real and the price is honest.

## Brand Personality

Local, trustworthy, unhurried. Three words: grounded, alive, dependable.
Emotional goals: confidence that the booking will go through, delight at discovering something authentic, comfort in a platform that feels like it was built for this region not imported from elsewhere.

## Anti-references

- Booking.com's corporate-blue, badge-heavy, urgency-manipulating UI ("Only 2 left!") — borrow the structure, not the dark patterns.
- Generic Western SaaS travel apps that feel disconnected from East Africa.
- Overcrowded dashboards with too many promo banners, countdown timers, and pop-ups.

## Design Principles

1. **Structure borrowed, soul kept** — Use Booking.com's proven two-column search layout and sticky booking widget but let imagery, copy, and spacing carry a distinctly local, unhurried character.
2. **Earn trust, don't manufacture urgency** — Price clearly, show real reviews, no fake scarcity tactics.
3. **Mobile first, desktop refined** — Tourists browse on phones; the desktop layout is a luxury, not the primary surface.
4. **Familiar patterns, zero confusion** — Standard navigation, predictable affordances. The interface disappears; the service is what the traveler remembers.
5. **Sparse enough to breathe** — East African landscapes are vast and open. The UI should have room, not be crammed.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Support for variable text sizes. High-contrast text on imagery. Touch targets ≥ 44px.
